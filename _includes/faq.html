FAQ

How long do gel nails last?
Gels will last 2 to 4 weeks depending on how well you take care of your hands. I recommend applying a hand cream or moisturiser at least twice daily and wearing protective gloves if cleaning, gardening and decorating. After about 2 weeks you may notice the growth of your own nails.
Are gels bad for my nails?
Gel nails are safe for natural nails. The damage I often see is from poor removal technique I've seen some nasty burns to nail beds, thinning and disfiguremnt of nails caused by picking or scratching the gel off and from excessive buffing.

Not only is nail damage painful, it needs time to heal before anything can be done to the damaged nail.
How do you remove my gel nails?
When done correctly, removing gel from your natural nail is perfectly safe and harm free. During removal, the top coat will be gently buffed off, acetone will be applied to cotton pads on the nail and sealed with foil for 10 to 15 minutes to soften the gel. I then gently remove the gel from your nail, buff with a soft buffer and finish with a new set of gels or apply a strengthener and oil to your own nail.

It's in my interest to help keep your natural nails healthy as it makes working with them easier the next time you come to get them done.
What is Polydip?
This is the latest trend in nails. The new technique involves brushing a gel base on the natural nail and dipping it into a special powder. The benefits of the dip powder technique is that there is no odour & no liquids. This means that the application process is quicker and simpler than the other methods, and removal is also easier.

Dip powder nails look natural and the powder actually provides more strength than gels. Because nothing is glued to your natural nails, your nail bed remains healthy and undamaged.
What are the differences with lashes like: "Russian Volume", "Hybrid" or "Classic"?
Russian Volume
Russian Volume lashes are made up of fans of 2 to 6 (2D to 6D) lightweight cosmetic lashes applied to individual natural lashes. The false lashes are thinner and lighter (often referred to as "mink lashes") than those used for other lash treatments. Russian Volume lashes use less glue and require less frequent infill treatment.
Classic
Classic lashes are applied 1 to 1 to your natural lashes resulting in a very natural look. Classic lashes are thicker than those used in "Russian Volume" treatments to give higher definition to each individual lash.
Hybrid
Hybrid lashes are popular in the celebrity world. They are a combination of "Classic" and "Russian Volume" lashes. Hybrid lashes can be used to create a highly personalised, individual look.
How do you remove my false lashes?
False lash removal takes time and care so as to preserve your own natural lashes. I use a specialised lash remover which is applied to the false lashes for 5 to 10 minutes, the false lashes are then gently brushed away leaving your natural lashes intact.

Many people try self removal at home with various oils, this is a lengthy process and often results in loss of your own lashes in the process.
